## The Rotary and the 2004 Tsunami

The tsunami that devastated Asia on Sunday, December 26, 2004, brought destruction to the coastal areas of southern Thailand. The Rotary Club Pattaya Marina immediately mobilized to launch a relief project for the village of Ban Nam Kem, a fishing community located 50 km from Khao Lak and 130 km north of Phuket Island.

Ban Nam Kem had 5,000 residents, with over 1,300 deaths and 1,000 missing persons reported.

### Phase 1 - February 13, 2005

Restoration of 4 fishing boats with equipment valued at **475,380 baht**, including motors, nets, refrigerated containers, and navigation equipment.

### Phase 2 - June 18, 2005

Distribution of fishing materials and two side-cars (value: **550,000 baht**) to enable four additional boats to resume operations. Medications were also provided to Takuapa Hospital.

### Impact

- Over **1,000,000 baht** in funds collected
- 8 fishing boats restored to service
- Medical supplies provided to the local hospital
- Demonstration of Rotary International's rapid response capability
